🌍 Overview: What is the Family Bible?

The Family Bible is not a linear script. It's a genealogical document—the inherited wounds, inherited wisdom, and inherited choices that ripple across 7 timelines and multiple dimensions.

Like a family Bible that records births, deaths, marriages, and curses passed down through generations, this document tracks:

  • Character arcs across timelines (Tyra → Bertha is the spine)
  • Emotional life stages mapped to the 9-point Kongo cosmogram
  • Scenes that define the story in real-time convergence (penultimate + climax)
  • The audience choice that determines which timeline survives
  • Character betrayals, healings, and transformations
Core Principle: The 9-point Kongo cosmogram is not geography. It's a map of emotional and spiritual development that anyone can apply to their own life, their family, their community, their journey.

🔄 The 9-Point Life Cycle

Each of the 9 points represents a phase of human and spiritual development. They are not meant to be linear—people spiral through them, return to them, live in multiple phases simultaneously.

1. EAST (The Child)

Entry Point — 2026 Story

Time Energy: A Time! Discovery. Total dependence on at least one other person.

Who Lives Here: Tyra begins here (emotionally, despite being 30+ years old). Future McFerrin (literal child, but also spiritual infant—just discovering fire).

Emotional Signature: Wonder mixed with terror. Infinite vulnerability. The need to be held.

2. NORTH (The Adult)

Movement Upward — Learning the Grind

Time Energy: Do or Die Time. Learning to love the rhythm and contours of your body, your social body, your life in it. Adapting emotionally to rapid change with far fewer guides.

Who Lives Here: Tyra at Spellman, ASU, and Trenholm. The Travelers. Community organizers. Peppaman (the healer who learned the hard way).

Emotional Signature: Fierce. Irridescent. Feral yet still. Building something that lasts.

3. WEST (The Elder)

Turning Inward — Reckoning Time

Time Energy: Reckoning Time. Some days believing it is a joy to have been alive and remain so. Other days consumed with every negative emotion and physical ailment possible.

Paradox: Either your decline is invisible (anti-elder prejudice: "you don't look sick") which means less aggression but also less assistance. OR you look different enough to transgress social expectations and experience greater aggression but potentially greater assistance.

Who Lives Here: Ms. Ora. Peppaman (especially in his later relationship with Tyra). Elliot (living a very long life).

Emotional Signature: Wisdom without guarantee it will be heard. The ability to hold contradictions. Family reckoning.

4. SOUTH (The Spirit/Ancestor Realm)

Grounding/Foundation — Between Time

Time Energy: Between Time. Constant. Unconquerable. Wild.

What Happens Here: Time and space travel are as common as death and birth. The travelers move through these unseen realms. What do they tell us? Why? What do we ask them that we're afraid to ask ourselves? How do they experience our pedantic yearning for loving parental figures (over and over and over again)?

Who Lives Here: The ancestors. The Night Gods. The ones who died in the bombing. The ones who chose to stay and guide.

5. CENTER/CROSSROADS

The Nexus Point — All Time / No Time

Where Everything Meets. This is where the penultimate scene happens. Where all characters are in different locations but spiritually and temporally synchronized. Where Big Kitty meets Bertha in a pocket dimension that feels like many years but is 30-45 minutes.

6. LOWER (Total Lower Chakra)

Finding Beauty in Horror — Demon Time

Where Bertha has been. The place of absolute servitude, absolute breaking, absolute forgetting of self. But also the place where transformation becomes possible because there is nothing left to lose.

7. HIGHER (Transcendence)

Finding Beauty in Divinity — Holy Time

Where Narissa lives. Year 3176+. A world where the choices made in 2026 have rippled into new forms of being. Where root vegetables are sentient communities. Where humans and non-humans negotiate existence together.

8. CENTER/CROSSROADS (Return)

The Nexus Point — An' Time

And Time. The moment after choice, before consequence. The space where you realize your decision will echo forever but you still have to keep living.

9. ZIG-ZAG/SQUIGGLE

The Spiral Back — WTF Time

Time Energy: Puzzling. Shocking. Confusing. Clarifying. The audience chooses how Elliot's council votes.

If they vote to STOP the quantum relay: They don't get bombed. The Crossroads Coalition (scientists, children, artists, night god guilds) collectively solve the problem through relay systems (pinball energy that bounces and passes, like a cypher, like a freestyle, like passing the torch rather than exploding at one point).

If they vote to ALLOW the quantum relay: Elliot disaffiliates from the guild and becomes an earth-firster (the least savvy, most chaotic people). They can only host a street play called "Narissa's Home" the night before Earth's implosion. Not a distraction. Just a beautiful, heartfelt performance of the very scenes we've been experiencing. They know there is no tomorrow. So being elsewhere is the same as being right here. Together. Scene.

💔 Tyra Washington's Complete Arc

The Foundational Wound

Tyra has always felt off around Peppaman. Since childhood. She cries more easily around him. Ms. Ora notices this but trusts Peppa deeply with vulnerable people, so she feels conflicted about his perceived rejection.

Why Tyra feels this: She is spiritually, emotionally, and dimensionally attuned to something in Peppaman that relates to Bertha. She doesn't have words for it. Her nervous system knows before her mind does.

The Escape (North Phase)

Tyra's Educational Journey

Trenholm State Teachers College: First time away from Montgomery. "She feels 10 feet taller. She can fly. She is fierce and irridescent. Her mind is feral yet still."

Why leaving heals her: It's not just Peppaman. It's Montgomery itself. Downtown especially makes her dread being there. She feels like she's suffocating.

Spellman College, then ASU: The pattern continues. Away = freedom. Away = life-giving. Away = she can think clearly about herself.

The Return (West Phase Begins)

She decides to come home. This decision felt bigger than her.

When she returns, something shifts. She feels lighter and less anxious around her father. She associates her father more with TOWN culture and Peppaman's art legacy. "The TOWN culture and Her Dad and the city are all intertwined for her. Or were."

She still feels the dread. But she feels a different level of hope. "She finds new life (literally) and new ability to create new spaces and revamp old relationships."

She is pregnant. About 12 weeks by June 2026.

The Reckoning Scene (Penultimate Convergence)

Tyra's Last Hour Before Midnight
Peppaman's house, then Jamal's car, driving toward Holy Ground

As they get closer to midnight on 6/20/2026, Tyra feels an urge to visit her father. She finds Peppaman and just wants to hold him and rub his feet.

She prays with him. Laughs with him. Thanks him for looking out for Big Kitty over the years.

Then she goes outside and sits in the car. While Jamal talks with Peppaman inside, Tyra feels something shifting. She feels less and less present. More and more pulled elsewhere.

"She feels fully exposed and terrified. She pulls her hoodie over her face and leans her car all the way back. She wonders if she's having pain related to the baby."

What's actually happening: Big Kitty has just met Bertha in the chamber. Bertha is remembering. Bertha is healing. Bertha is about to make a choice. And Tyra, who IS connected to Bertha across dimensions, can feel it happening.

The Moment of Redistribution

In the moment when Big Kitty finally reaches Bertha and they meet after so long, Bertha remembers Tyra/Narissa. She remembers the drone bombs that are about to drop. She realizes she has minutes to decide: use her power for destruction (she has every right) or redistribute it to those closest to Big Kitty's heart.

Bertha chooses redistribution.

In that split second, Tyra feels it. "She begins feeling much better, and that's when she explains to Jamal that she is pregnant."

She goes from looking like she's having heroin withdrawal (which Jamal suspects, knowing her history with harder drugs) to suddenly present, alive, capable.

Tyra at the Climax

They pull up to Ms. Ora's. Find her on the back porch with Bunny, recorder still rolling. Squirrel arrives moments later. Five people trying to shout over each other.

Then: BOOM! BOOM! BOOM! BOOM! BOOM! (pause) BOOM! BOOM!

334 seconds of bombing. The capitol. The interstate. Liberty and all around there.

Tyra throws up in Jamal's car. They're down a seat and can't take Squirrel. Squirrel offers to drive. By doing so, he can transport more stuff. He has the lojack.

Tyra leaves Montgomery. She doesn't know if she'll come back.

🐉 Bertha & Big Kitty: The Healing

Who Bertha Is

A bound dragon. Imprisoned since 1901 when she was captured and forced to bind her infinite energy into a single geographic location (Alabama). Her power has fueled the political hierarchy for 125+ years. But the binding broke her mind.

She forgot who she was. She believed the lies they told her:

  • That her own people had rebelled and cast her down
  • That she deserved to be enslaved as punishment for their pride
  • That her only way to atone was to protect their children
  • That if she didn't feed them, they would die
  • That her loved ones would be melted away without her protection

She came to believe: "I'm not ok / You're ok."

Big Kitty's Role

The only person Bertha can trust to help her remember herself.

Big Kitty finds Bertha in the chamber. She is almost incapable of speaking. A shell. But Kitty has been preparing for this moment. He knows how to coax her back.

In what feels to Kitty like many years, but is only 30-45 minutes in real time, they have a long and sweet friendship.

This is crucial: They are never romantic. Kitty can't let go of Tyra/Yemmy. Bertha is learning to love herself and figure out who she is. Kitty doesn't want that to be rooted in him. Bertha is hostile to the idea. But Kitty's gentleness and intrigue with her is what helps her recover her actual story and self.

Bertha's Final Choice

By the time Big Kitty reaches her, Bertha has moved through the emotional stages:

Entry
"I'm not ok / You're ok"
With Kitty
"I'm ok / You're not ok"
Healing Deepens
"I'm very ok / You're definitely not ok"
Final Wisdom
"I'm ok / You're ok"

In that final moment of clarity, Bertha realizes she has minutes before the bombs drop. She could use her power for destruction (justified, deserved, necessary). But instead:

She chooses to redistribute her energy to the loved ones closest to Big Kitty's heart.

This redistribution is what saves Tyra from a crisis. What allows Jamal to drive safely. What gives the entire group the spiritual/emotional energy to survive the next 48 hours.

After Bertha Ascends

When Big Kitty emerges from the chamber, everything is different. "He is older. He is moving slower." He has lived a full life with Bertha, even if it was only minutes in external time. He looks at the governors' portraits in the rotunda and then sees his own reflection. "He is surprised to see his age, but delighted."

He sits on the capitol steps and enjoys the sunshine. In Bertha's timeline, Big Kitty has been living inside her consciousness for as many days/years as the number of amendments in Alabama's constitution (the longest in the world). When he finally emerges, he has lived a lifetime.

He carries Bertha's gift with him for the rest of the story.

🎭 The Penultimate Convergence Scene

This is the crucial scene that precedes the climax. Every major character is in a different physical location, but they are all spiritually synchronized. What happens here determines how they navigate what comes next.

Real-Time Multi-Location Convergence

Location 1: Peppaman's House
Downtown Montgomery

Jamal is talking with Peppaman inside. Tyra is in the car outside, feeling the effects of Bertha's redistribution ripple through her body.

Location 2: The Crossroads (I-65)
Exit 169

Future McFerrin is getting set up to do music with local artists and neighborhood residents. The voice of the uprising, covered in Peppaman's blood (who died shielding Future from a drone blast).

Location 3: The Sanctuary
432 Goldthwaite Street

Squirrel is being questioned/grilled about his betrayal. The tech he sold. The information he gave up. His desperation to be seen and feared rather than loved.

Location 4: Montgomery Country Club
Ritual Space

Phat Tony, Lil Alex, Unique, Dominique, and other Night Gods are about to start the fully costumed Root Veggie Tails + Dragon/Castle show. A live dramatization of the vegan future they're choosing. A preview of what Narissa's world will be.

Location 5: Ms. Ora's Kitchen
Her back porch

Ms. Ora and Bunny are sitting at the kitchen table. Bunny has a recorder rolling. Ms. Ora is tapping into her vision gifts as the tech they were using to speak with Big Kitty begins to fail.

Bunny has to narrate and describe Bertha's prison-dialysis room with uncanny accuracy. Sometimes describing wherever Tyra is. Eventually describing Jamal and Tyra driving toward Hope Hull.

The Tech Failure

About 20 minutes before Big Kitty reaches Bertha's chamber, the tech fails. The direct communication line goes down. From this point forward, everything relies on Bunny's vision gift and Ms. Ora's spiritual attunement.

This is why Bunny's narration is so crucial. She is the bridge between the Bertha dimension and the material world. She is describing what she "sees" — the events in real-time, even though she's never been to that location.

The Function of This Scene

Every character-defining action and story-defining action has its most fundamental expression here. What each character does in this moment determines their trajectory in the climax and the endings.

  • Tyra: Moves from crisis (physical/emotional) to clarity. Learns she's pregnant.
  • Jamal: Releases his faith in Peppaman. Becomes the driver. Takes action.
  • Ms. Ora: Activates her vision gift fully. Becomes the bridge.
  • Bunny: Honors her role. Narrates what she cannot logically know.
  • Squirrel: Faces his choices. Offers to help.
  • Future McFerrin: Continues the music. Holds the space.
  • Big Kitty & Bertha: Meet. Heal. Choose redistribution.

⚡ The Climax: 334 Seconds of Bombing

The Convergence

They pull up to Ms. Ora's. Everyone is suddenly in one location for the first time. The penultimate scene has prepared them. Now they must act together.

The Scene
Ms. Ora's back porch, nighttime

Squirrel pulls up moments after Tyra and Jamal arrive.

Five people are attempting to shout or talk over each other. Tyra just threw up in Jamal's car. They're down a seat. They can't take Squirrel.

In that moment of chaos—

The Attack

BOOM! BOOM! BOOM! BOOM! BOOM!
(pause)
BOOM! BOOM!
And this continues with varying rates of velocity for a full 334 seconds.

The capitol building. The interstate. Liberty and all around there. The initial wave is 5 bombs. Then a pause (2-3 seconds of silence where everyone's heart stops). Then 2 more. Then irregular bursts.

A full 334 seconds. About 5 minutes and 34 seconds of bombing. Long enough that people start to wonder if it will ever stop. Long enough that they make decisions about their lives.

The Aftermath (Immediate)

They check cell phones. News reports. Talk to neighbors.

The capitol has been hit. The interstate. Downtown. "Why through there?" Phat Tony calls. "We had to cut through Spring Valley and Snoudon but we're en route to Holy Ground."

They hear: sounds like police shooting. Someone was shooting with the police. Or someone shot AT the police. The city has been placed under attack.

They only guess who. (The Christian nationalist extremists hijacking military jets, aided by law enforcement and paramilitary groups, seeking to start a holy war.)

The Decision

They need more room. Tyra threw up. They can't take Squirrel. Squirrel offers to drive. By doing so, he can transport more stuff. He has the lojack (a tracking device they may or may not disclose to authorities).

This moment — Squirrel's offer to help despite knowing he betrayed them — is his path toward redemption or damnation, depending on what he does next.

🐿️ Squirrel: Betrayal & Consequence

The Setup (Before Climax)

Squirrel gave up information about Big Kitty and the dragon to a reporter for Shake the Field Media who was flirting with him. She seemed stuck on wanting to know more about the Night Gods.

Squirrel was high. He wanted to show her that he and Big Kitty were different. "There are things Kitty won't do, that I will and have. If I were doing the night gods, I would have done XYZ."

Why he betrayed: He needed to be seen. To be feared. To be respected separately from Kitty. "Kitty is the sweetest man in this town. And I am feared. Both respected. For a reason."

The Reporter's True Purpose

The reporter was more than a flirt. She was intelligence. Someone working to determine if Squirrel could be "turned for or against their trans-humanist interests."

During their conversation, they discussed transhumanism. She used examples of Beta Israel discrimination in Ethiopia, Israel, Palestine, and UK to argue: "Humans are consistently full of shit. This is why we should improve our humanity with technology that can ultimately solve for death itself."

This message planted a seed. Maybe there is a technological solution. Maybe transcendence is possible. Maybe Squirrel's hunger to be seen, to be feared, to be transcendent — maybe it can be solved by the right technology.

Squirrel's Crisis

After the bombing, Squirrel is in danger. He knows the reporter wasn't just a reporter. He knows he's proximity to Peter Rabbit interests (who he doesn't like). He sexualized the woman, so he was less censored around her. He told her too much.

The law enforcement people isolate this clip. The clip where Squirrel talks about what the people need. "The folks selling parking on their land. We feed off each other."

Out of context, it sounds like Squirrel was in on something. Like he was part of the planning. He becomes a suspect.

Squirrel's Offer at the Climax

Despite knowing he's in danger. Despite knowing he betrayed. Despite being high and afraid. Squirrel offers to drive.

This is his moment of choosing. Is he going to run? Or is he going to help? Is he going to lean into his reputation as someone feared? Or is he going to become someone trusted?

For now, he chooses to help. But the reporter, the technology, the promise of transcendence through fear and power — those seeds are still planted. Squirrel will carry them forward.

🌈 The Choice Point: Two Possible Endings

After the climax, after they drive away, after they reach the Holy Ground safe, they gather. The community gathers. And they must decide.

The Question

The quantum relay station that Peter Rabbit and the scientists built — should they activate it or destroy it?

What it does: Harnesses all of Bertha's distributed energy into one location. Uses that energy to open a portal off-planet. Allows humans to begin exploring the cosmos. But requires the destruction/annihilation/domination of whatever is at that location (it's hidden but it's something alive).

What's at risk: If they activate it, people die. But humanity gets a chance to explore the cosmic order. If they stop it, Elliot's quantum relay council survives and remains unified. But the deep hunger for cosmic transcendence remains unsolved.

🔴 ENDING 1: Stop the Relay (Audience Votes NO)

Immediate Consequence

The activists, scientists, and artists win. Peter Rabbit's plan fails. The quantum relay is not activated. No one is bombed at that location.

The Genius Solution

The Crossroads Coalition (scientists, children, artists, night god guilds) devises an alternative.

Instead of a relay that requires total annihilation at one point, they create a PINBALL RELAY. A system that:

  • Bounces the signal from planet to planet
  • Passes the freestyle in the cypher (like music, like improv)
  • Allows the gravitational fields of other celestial bodies to hold it and grow it
  • Builds the energy without violating any core rights
  • Discovers pocket dimensions (unassuming places, like the shotgun houses in the story)
  • Produces optimal outcomes without producing toxic byproducts
The Future

Elliot lives a very long life. Humanity learns to work with root vegetable sentient communities. Intermarrying becomes fashionable. Elliot eventually becomes a Simon Says toy — almost ceremonial. His grandchildren lead uprisings. One moderator runs off with an inspiring co-organizer of a massage caste of root veggies. Elliot has many comments per day but misses that child.

Elliot dies as the grandparent of the lowest caste root vegetable who leads their uprising. The genealogy continues. Narissa is born. The root vegetables sing.

🔵 ENDING 2: Allow the Relay (Audience Votes YES)

Immediate Consequence

The relay activates. The energy is released. Humanity gains access to the cosmos. But Elliot disaffiliates from the guild.

Elliot's Spiral

Less active as a guild member. More active with earth-firsters. "The least savvy and nuanced thinkers available in Elliot's world. The Bad News Bears full of all kinds of can't get right."

The earth-firsters can only host a street play called "Narissa's Home" the night before Earth's scheduled implosion. Not a distraction. Not a cover for a larger plot. Just a beautiful, heartfelt performance of the very scenes we've been experiencing.

The Irony

They know there is no tomorrow. So being elsewhere is the same as being right here. Together. The play happens on June 19, whatever year that is. The night before Earth's implosion. It's a concert. A gathering. A last testimony.

"We know there is no tomorrow. So being elsewhere is the same as being right here."

Narissa's Paradox

Narissa still exists in this ending. But as a ghost. As a possibility. As the child that might have been born if Tyra survived Earth's implosion. The story we're telling is the story of what would have been. The performance is a requiem.

The Meta-Truth

This is not a story where the "right choice" is obvious. Both endings are tragic. Both are hopeful. Both require sacrifice.

The audience chooses not because they're told which is right, but because they understand the values and they vote for the world they want to inhabit. Or the world they think they deserve.

The beauty is: We experience both endings. The play IS both timelines. When you watch "Narissa's Home," you're experiencing Tyra and Jamal and Bunny and the entire circle's journey IN BOTH REALITIES SIMULTANEOUSLY.

🎭 Note on Phat Tony's Show

What Phat Tony does at the Montgomery Country Club: A live dramatization of the Root Veggie Tails (the vegan future they're choosing) plus elements of the high school play featuring Bertha's story.

This show happens during the penultimate convergence scene. It's a preview of the possible future. A ritual. A spell. A way of speaking things into existence.

The Night Gods (and Phat Tony) understand that you can't just kill the old world and hope a new one grows. You have to sing the new world into being while the old one is still smoking.

⚔️ The Political Antagonists (To Humanize)

President Gump, AG Major, Montrose, and Senator Coach are the current faces of the Christian nationalist regime. But their stories are complicated. They are people who believe they are protecting something. They are afraid. They are making choices they think are necessary.

This does not excuse their choices. But understanding their logic makes the story richer. It's not good vs. evil. It's competing visions of what humans are capable of and what we should be allowed to become.

The Crossroads

Characters & Conflicts

The people at the center of the story and the forces arrayed against them

The Core Circle

Big Kitty (Charles Mason McCall VI)

Sound Seer · Released Liberation
Autistic. Bipolar. Lost both parents to the carceral system (father killed at Holman in 2001; mother murdered at Tutwiler in 2003). Raised by his grandmother Bunny. He hears sound at quantum depth. He sees only black and white but perceives the world through frequency and tone. Spent 2021-2023 locked in Jefferson Davis Hospital after a psychiatric commitment became criminal. In the hospital law library, he learned about his family's deep connection to the binding mechanism beneath the Capitol. He wrote three words on his palm: Interstate, Conviction, Removal. On June 20, 2026, at 9:30 AM, he descended into the tunnels beneath the Capitol to confront Bertha. He died in those tunnels when explosives detonated, but not before releasing the dragon.

Tyra Yemeshia Washington

Scholar · Performer · Roving Correspondent
The most beloved person in this story. Graduate degrees in engineering and film. Raises her daughter Narissa. Pregnant during the occupation (didn't know it yet). Partner to Big Kitty. Conducts the prayer offering at dawn on June 20. Leads performances that weave theology, memory, and political critique into the occupation narrative. Leads the southern diaspora to Bahia, Brazil after the strike. Writes everything down—poems, songs, testimonies—to preserve what happened. Mother of Narissa.

Jamal Washington

Radio Host · Archivist · Keeper of the Record
Editor of this Bible. Trans man (born Braelyn Jamyla Washington). Chose his name after Bryan Stevenson. Runs WZRD 77.7 FM from the Sanctuary at Jubilee. During the occupation, he broadcasts continuously—turning local testimony and music into citywide signal. Preserves the archives obsessively. Evacuates all recordings per Peppaman's instructions. His tapes become the primary historical source for Elliot's 3176 Transmission. Carries Tyra's voice and Bunny's memory southward.

Phat Tony (Tony Pham)

Craftsman · Logistics · Practical Magic
Vietnamese American. Grew up around the corner from Big Kitty's grandmother Bunny. Works in practical SFX and illusion. Builds the physical infrastructure of the occupation. Leads the Country Club disruption on June 20. Escapes north and west through the lower-caste streets. Eventually builds a cooperative in British Columbia. His gift is making systems work where they're not supposed to.

Squirrel (Seth Taylor)

Rationalist · Organizer · Survivor
LAMP Class of 2009. Embedded in the Mayor's office through a fellowship. Carries the weight of having taken Big Kitty to Jefferson Davis Hospital in 2021—a decision that haunts him. Captured after the occupation and held atop King Williams Apartments. Rescued later by Squirrel and Unique. Dies in the Battle for Huntsville in 2032.

Peppaman (Booker Washington)

Father · Witness · Oracle
Jamal's father. Autistic Army veteran. Stone Street counselor and oracle. Sits on his porch for decades, listening, mapping the world. Never leaves. Then on June 20, 2026, at dawn, he leaves the porch for the first time in years and walks to the Crossroads to stand with the village. He is killed in the first nonlethal strikes. His last act is ensuring the archives are evacuated. His porch becomes legendary. His tapes become the foundation of the data trust.

Elliot John Henry McCall

Descendant · Historian · Witness from 3176
Born approximately 3138. Descendant of Big Kitty. In the year 3176, he stands before the Planning Guild of Freezone 4 and presents the story of the Crossroads occupation. He argues that this story proves something about community, resistance, and the cost of liberation. He is not neutral—he carries the DNA and the tapes of his ancestors. His 14-day presentation is based solely on documented sources: recordings, testimonies, songs, social media. His argument is that the Crossroads occupation must be remembered as evidence that protecting the home by desecrating the home is not an option.

Bunny (Big Kitty's Grandmother)

Matriarch · Keeper of the Rusted Sword
Charles Mason McCall VI's paternal grandmother. Raised Big Kitty after his parents' deaths. Holds the rusted sword and the archive of the family's political memory. Participates in the occupation. Flees south with Tyra and Jamal. Carries the chamber song. Dies in 2030 of grief, sixteen months after Lil Alex's death. Her voice and her presence are felt throughout the narrative as the anchor to pre-occupation memory.

The Antagonist Forces

The Two Networks

The antagonists operate through two overlapping networks:

  • Elder Wing (Hierarchy & Caste): Represented by property assessors, police, political structures, and religious institutions that benefit from maintaining control through voter suppression.
  • Younger Wing (Transhumanism & Extraction): Represented by tech firms, venture capitalists, and AI developers who want to leave Earth and commodify everything—including Black Belt knowledge and genomes.

They cooperate on one thing: keeping Bertha bound. They disagree on everything else.

The Louisiana v. Calais Legal Crisis (2024-2026)

The occupation happens at the exact moment the Supreme Court's Section 2 Voting Rights protections hang in the balance. A case called Louisiana v. Calais threatens to erase federal voting rights protections entirely. The occupation is, in part, an emergency response to this imminent threat.

Bertha (The Bound Dragon)

Trapped Energy · Grief Made Power
A being who arrived in Montgomery before Alabama became a state. Already grieving—carrying a loss so vast it has no name. In 1901, Alabama's power brokers bound Bertha beneath the Capitol. They did not kill her. They trapped her tremendous energy to power an empire built on caste, control, and the compression of possibility. Bertha's power flows through electric grids, prison systems, pharmaceutical distribution, and computational networks. Her energy is metabolized through ritual on Alabama statehood day, Jefferson Davis's birthday, and winter solstice. She has been bound for 125 years. On June 20, 2026, Big Kitty releases her. What follows changes everything.

Major Conflicts

Voting Rights vs. Voter Suppression

The Voting Rights Act Section 2 protections have been under attack since Shelby v. Holder (2013). Louisiana v. Calais (pending 2026) threatens to erase them entirely. The occupation is a direct response to this political emergency. The question: Can the federal government protect the right to vote, or will it be returned entirely to the states to suppress?

Political Extraction vs. Community Sovereignty

The Crossroads occupation argues that communities have the right to define the value of their own neighborhoods, their own labor, their own knowledge. This conflicts with systems that extract that value upward—property assessments that devalue Black neighborhoods, census counts that erase people, data harvesting that trains AI to predict and control rather than liberate.

Infrastructure for the Few vs. Infrastructure for the Many

The antagonist networks want to build a quantum relay station that sacrifices Earth for the benefit of an elite few. The occupation models an alternative: local mesh networks, community data trusts, cooperative energy systems, shared knowledge that stays in community hands. The story argues: Which infrastructure will we build? For whom? Who gets to decide?

The Cost of Resistance

The story does not shy away from violence. Peppaman is killed. Big Kitty dies. Homes are destroyed. Schools are bombed. The story asks: What does it cost to resist? Can we refuse both complicity and revenge? How do we hold that tension? This is not a triumphalist narrative. It is an honest one.

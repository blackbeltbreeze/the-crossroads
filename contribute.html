The Crossroads

How to Contribute

Be part of building The Crossroads across five modalities

The Crossroads is not a finished product. It is a living archive being built by a community. We are seeking collaborators—writers, musicians, visual artists, organizers, technologists, educators—who want to help bring the story into the world across multiple formats.

We operate on a data trust model. Every person who contributes—whether paid directly or through revenue sharing—becomes part of the documented record. You own your work. If this work earns money, revenue is shared fairly among all contributors.

Five Modalities · Where We Need Help

Writing & Narrative

Complete final gaps in the Story Bible. Draft the frame device (Elliot's 3176 presentation). ~60 hours of focused editorial work.

  • Pay: $2,500-$5,000
  • You own: 100% copyright of your work
  • Revenue share: 15% if work is sold
  • We seek: Black radical storytelling, complicated spirituality, speculative fiction

Sound & Music

Audio drama production, sound design, mixing, mastering. Integration of Smoke & Mirrors album with narrative.

  • Pay: $2,000-$7,500 (varies by scope)
  • You own: 100% copyright of your recordings
  • Revenue share: 15% of streaming/sales
  • We seek: Hip-hop, blues, gospel, jazz, electronic traditions. Quantum/spiritual sonic textures.

Visual & Design

Photography series, animation, street musical design, visual style guide.

  • Pay: $2,000-$5,000 (varies by scope)
  • You own: 100% copyright of images/designs
  • Revenue share: 15% if images licensed/sold
  • We seek: Montgomery-based or Black Belt photographers. Documentary realism + speculative imagination.

Action & Organizing

Community workshops, AR scavenger hunt, skill-up apps, toolkit development. Grassroots production resources.

  • Pay: $1,500-$4,000 (varies by scope)
  • You own: Tools & curriculum can be freely distributed
  • Revenue share: Participation bonus if tool is licensed
  • We seek: Organizers, technologists, educators with community respect. No extraction.

Festival & Convention

Feasibility study, partnership architecture, event production planning for Phase 1 festival launch.

  • Pay: $2,500-$6,000 (varies by scope)
  • You own: Partnership frameworks can be replicated
  • Revenue share: 15% of festival proceeds
  • We seek: Festival producers with experience in Black Belt, rural, post-industrial communities.

Payment Model & Equity

Option A: Commissioned Work

$1,500-$5,000 upfront depending on scope and experience + 15% revenue share if work is sold/distributed.

You retain 100% copyright. We don't sell your work without your agreement. Annual accounting if revenue comes in.

Option B: Collaborative Partnership

Monthly stipend ($500-$1,500) + housing/travel if needed + 20-30% revenue share if work is sold.

You're part of the core creative team. You own your work 100%. You have say in how work is used.

Option C: Volunteer / Trade

No upfront payment, but you get free access to everything we build, prominent credit, and 15% revenue share if work earns.

For people who believe in the work and want to be part of building it without immediate financial exchange.

What We Promise

  • Your name and work are credited everywhere. In the story, in the metadata, in annual reports.
  • You own your master work. Recordings, images, writing—100% yours. We can't license without your permission.
  • Revenue is shared fairly. If we earn a grant or revenue from sales, you get a documented share.
  • You get first right to sell elsewhere. We don't restrict your work or claim exclusive rights.
  • Annual accounting if revenue comes. Transparency about money in and money out.
  • You become part of the data trust. Your contribution is documented forever. Your descendants inherit rights to the work.

What We Ask

  • 12-18 month commitment. Some flexibility, but this is a serious build, not a sprint.
  • Honor Black Belt voice and complicated spirituality. Don't flatten or extract. Work with integrity.
  • Political honesty. This story features violence, rage, and grief. Don't glamorize or shy away. Examine.
  • Willingness to be part of something bigger. This isn't just a paycheck. It's building proof of concept for Phase 2/3/4 infrastructure.

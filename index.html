What is The Crossroads?

On Juneteenth 2026, a circle of friends from Montgomery occupy Interstate 65 across four exits to expose a bound dragon named Bertha whose trapped energy has powered Alabama's political hierarchy since 1901. The occupation lasts 72 hours. It ends in drone strikes. It triggers a national uprising. It echoes across 1,150 years.

This is a story about the costs of power and the costs of resistance. It is told across multiple formats: as written narrative, as sound, as visual media, as lived practice, as a festival, and as a data trust.

It is a story about you.

The Phase 1 Vision

Phase 1 involves completing the writing of a 4-part story and producing it across multiple modalities:

  • Narrative: Novella or limited-series script
  • Audio: Radio drama, studio album (Smoke & Mirrors), sound collages
  • Digital: Interactive website and map, augmented reality scavenger hunt
  • Visual: Photography exhibit, animated sequences, street musical
  • Action: Toolkit for organizers and educators, community workshops, skill-up apps
  • Festival: Annual gathering at the Sanctuary and across the Black Belt

The Smoke & Mirrors album is already complete. The Story Bible is locked. We're finishing the gaps and moving into production.

Built on Integrity

The Crossroads operates as a data trust. Every person who contributes—writing, music, visuals, organizing—earns documented credit and shared revenue if this work succeeds.

This is not charity. It is infrastructure. It models what it means for a community to own and benefit from the knowledge it produces.

The story itself demonstrates this: Elliot in 3176 cannot tell the story of 2026 without crediting every porch conversation, every WZRD tape, every person who showed up.

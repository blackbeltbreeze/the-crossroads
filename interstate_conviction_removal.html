Interstate: Mutual Aid & Cross-Border Organizing

An interstate is a system built for extraction. Interstates moved goods for profit, bypassed local economies, accelerated gentrification, separated communities from themselves. The Crossroads occupation took that extraction infrastructure and repurposed it as a gathering place.

Interstate organizing means: we don't organize within state boundaries only. We organize where the infrastructure connects us. We build mutual aid networks that flow across jurisdictions. We create redundancy so that if one node is destroyed, the network survives.

This is not new. Black communities have always organized across state lines—through the Underground Railroad, through gospel music networks, through migration patterns, through family ties that span the map. The Crossroads makes that visible.

Conviction: Voting Rights & Political Power

The Voting Rights Act Section 2 is the last federal protection ensuring that voting rights cannot be stripped by state-level gerrymandering and suppression. In 2024-2026, that protection hangs in the balance as Louisiana v. Calais works its way through the courts.

Conviction means: we don't compromise on voting rights. We don't accept "separate but equal" structures or reduced ballots. We draw the line. We say: voting rights are non-negotiable. This is the hill.

The Crossroads occupation happens the moment before the Supreme Court potentially guts Section 2. It is an emergency response. It says: something is being taken from us. We will not go quietly. We will occupy the infrastructure. We will make it impossible to ignore.

Removal: Building Alternative Systems

Removal is the most long-term work. It means: we stop asking the hostile system to let us survive. We build systems that don't depend on the system to approve them.

This includes: community energy microgrids that don't rely on privatized utilities; local data trusts that keep neighborhood information in neighborhood hands; land trusts that remove property from speculative markets; cooperative businesses that distribute wealth rather than extracting it; community gardening that produces food security; herbalism and traditional medicine that serve healing without pharmaceutical extraction; rootwork and spiritual practice that maintain ancestral wisdom.

Phase 2 of the Crossroads is literally the formalization of this infrastructure. It is not theoretical. It is being built right now in Montgomery.

The Eight Directions & 360-Degree Analysis

The Crossroads uses a cosmogram-based framework of eight directions for analyzing reality and making decisions. This replaces binary (two-choice) thinking with multi-directional (eight-direction) thinking.

Rather than "us vs. them" or "good vs. evil," the eight-direction framework asks: Where are we moving? Who moves with us? What are the costs? What is being built? What is being destroyed? What are the patterns? What do the ancestors say? What do the children need?

This framework is based in Black and Indigenous cosmologies—the Kongo cosmogram, African diaspora spiritual practice, and contemporary Black radical thought.
